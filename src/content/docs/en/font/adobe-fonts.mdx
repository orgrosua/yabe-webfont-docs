[Adobe Fonts](https://fonts.adobe.com) is a font subscription service offered by Adobe with over 20,000 fonts from hundreds of type foundries.

Despite Adobe [doesn't offer](https://helpx.adobe.com/fonts/using/font-licensing.html#web-host) the ability to locally host the web font files on your server, it's pretty easy to embed Adobe Fonts on your WordPress website with Yabe Webfont.

## Step 1: Get the Project ID

Visit [https://fonts.adobe.com/my_fonts](https://fonts.adobe.com/my_fonts#web_projects-section) and sign in to your account. Then copy the `Project ID` of the project you want to use.

<a data-fslightbox href="/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/1.png">
![](/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/1.png)
</a>

## Step 2: Open the Settings page

On your WordPress website, go to **Appearance → Yabe Webfont** and switch to the **Settings** tab.

<a data-fslightbox href="/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/2.png">
![](/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/2.png)
</a>

## Step 3: Sync the project

Paste the Project ID you copied in Step 1 into the **Adobe Fonts → Project ID** field and click the **Sync** button.

<a data-fslightbox href="/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/3.png">
![](/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/3.png)
</a>

## Step 4: Synced!

If the synchronization is successful, you will see a success message.
Switch to the **Fonts** tab, and the fonts in the project are synced will appear in the list.

<a data-fslightbox href="/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/4.png">
![](/tutorial/font/adobe-fonts/4.png)
</a>
